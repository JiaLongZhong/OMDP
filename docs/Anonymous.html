How to Browse the Web with Maximum Amount of Security:

Question: Why are we doing this?

Whenever you go to any website, your privacy maybe invade by your government or companies like Google and Facebook. The companies then sell your information they obtain from your devices to third-party organizations. Disclaimer: The more security you have the less convenient your life become, because you must do more work to become more secure. But everyone must be able to do the Basic Privacy.

Basic Privacy:

  • Password:

  • a) Strong Password: https://howsecureismypassword.net/ help you identify the strength of your password.

  • b) Secure question should not be answer truthfully: Hacker can search up your personal information that may deal with your security question.b) Secure question should not be answer truthfully: Hacker can search up your personal information that may deal with your security question.

  • c) Don’t use short PIN: use a password with symbol, characters, and numbers.

  • d) Use Password Manager: To keep track of your password and security question.

  • Two-Factor Authentication:

  • a) Password + Addition information (phone number, email, code from sites, etc.): This make it harder for hacker to get access to your information.

  • Your Web-Browser:

  • a) Only use trusted Add-on or extension. If it look sketchy don’t brother with it.

  • b) Use site like DuckDuckGO as search engine: Since it doesn’t store cookies and history cache.

  • VPN:

  • a) Get a secure VPN (Example: NordVPN): Make sure the VPN avoid Proxies. VPN encrypt your data, so no one can see what you are doing in the web.

  • Social Media Rules:

  • a) Hide real Information.

  • b) Do not talk to stranger.

  • c) Do not post on public comments.

  • d) Disable Permissions.

  • Phone Number & Smartphone & Computer:

  • a) Avoid posting phone number online as much as possible.

  • b) Di sable any setting that send your usage data to the apps and the operating system.

  • c) Active VPN at all time.

  • d) Disable Location on setting.

  • e) Disable Bluetooth when you’re not using it.

  • f) Make sure you uninstall any unneeded app or programs.
